Short Bio

Hi! — I'm Zixuan Zhang, an LLM developer, working on post-training, RL, and agentic capabilities for Rufus foundation models at Amazon.

I am a major contributor to the post-training and RL recipe design for Rufus model releases from 2024 to 2026, covering both in-house pre-trained and open-source base models, dense to ultra-sparse MoE architectures, and small, mid, to large model sizes. Specifically, I am leading the RL recipe design for mathematical / STEM reasoning and agentic coding, achieving state-of-the-art performances on both math tasks, python competitive coding, and terminal agent task solving. My work mainly focus on synthetic data generation, RL recipe development, and environment design and scaling across reasoning, coding, and other product-specific agentic applications in shopping scenarios.

I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from UIUC, advised by Prof. Heng Ji. I am honored to have my Ph.D. thesis Completing the knowledge lifecycle for language models advised by Prof. Chengxiang Zhai, Prof. Hanghang Tong, Dr. Kevin Small, and Dr. Scott Wen-tau Yih. Before that, I received my B.S. in Computer Science from Shanghai Jiao Tong University (SJTU), where I did deep reinforcement learning research under the supervision or Prof. Junchi Yan.
